Invited Speakers

Stephen von Tetzchner (University of Oslo, Norway)


Augmentative and Alternative Communication through the life span


14:00, Jan. 8. 2023

Stephen von Tetzchner is professor emeritus of developmental psychology and school psychology at the Department of Psychology, University of Oslo, Norway. His research includes a wide range of issues related to typical and atypical development in general, and communication and language development in particular, including the development of individuals with intellectual disabilities, motor impairment, deafness, blindness, Rett syndrome, Tourette syndrome and autism spectrum disorder. He has worked particularly with children who fail to acquire spoken language in the normal manner and may need alternative means of language to supplement or substitute for speech. He has addressed their development of communication and language from a developmental perspective, with a focus on the transactional processes that govern development. He has published a large number of scientific articles and textbooks on typical and atypical developmental psychology, language development, augmentative and alternative communication, rehabilitation, challenging behaviour, school psychology, Batten disease and autism spectrum disorder. In 2023, Stephen von Tetzchner received Communication Matters Research Award, Rehabilitation International Centennial Award for Significant Contributions and ISAAC Distinctive Service Award.
